"Forbidden Planet" in Neptunian Desert

Astronomers have used a desert-based observatory to identify an exoplanet that falls in the middle of what scientists had dubbed the Neptunian Desert. The newly discovered planet is formally known as NGTS-4b but nicknamed "The Forbidden Planet" for its supposed implausibility. The size is three time bigger than the Earth.

Red Moon

The next Red Moon will come on May 26, 2021 at North America, South America, Asia, Australia and the Pacific. It also known as Blood Moon. Red Moon is a full moon nearly always appears coppery red during a total lunar eclipse. That's because the dispersed light from all the Earth's sunrises and sunsets falls on the face of the moon at mid-eclipse.

Pink Planet

Named GJ 504b, the planet is made of pink gas. It’s similar to Jupiter, a giant gas planet in our own solar system. But GJ 504b is four times more massive. The planet you’re looking for is 4.65 billion miles away from its sun. Almost no light reaches it.

700 Trillion Suns

This newly observed object is known officially as SMSS J215728.21-360215 found on May of 2018. This black hole is growing so rapidly that it’s as luminous as 700 trillion suns, shining thousands of times more brightly than an entire galaxy due to all of the gases it sucks in daily that cause lots of friction and heat.

Quasar

Quasars are distant objects powered by black holes a billion times as massive as our sun. It is known as the brightest object in our universe that they eclipse the ancient galaxies that contain them.
