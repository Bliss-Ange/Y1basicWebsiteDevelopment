Meteor Shower


The Lyrid meteor shower passes by Earth every year around April 16-25. This year the peak is expected to be shortly before dawn on April 23rd. Sadly, bright light from the Moon will drown all but the very brightest meteors this year.

Mercury at Greatest Eastern Elongation


Look for the planet Mercury low above the western horizon after sunset on June 23; binoculars and an unobstructed view will certainly help! Low as it is, this is still the the highest position that Mercury can be spotted for 2019.

Jupiter Opposition


Earth will be between Jupiter and the Sun on June 10, meaning Jupiter is at opposition. On that date, Jupiter rises in the east as the Sun sets in the west, remaining visible the entire night. Jupiter will be one of the brightest objects in the night sky, shining at magnitude -2.6. Its four largest moons and cloud bands are easily spotted with even a small telescope.
